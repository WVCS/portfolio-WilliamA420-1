Primary Colors

The three most basic types of primary colors are red, yellow and blue. With these 3 colors you can form every other color of the rainbow. An example of the colors would be green, purple, and orange.

Secondary Colors

The secondary colors are colors what were created by primary colors. The three examples of these colors would be green, yellow, and purple

Complementary/Split-Complementary Colors

The complemantary colors are colors that are direct opposites of eachother. Examples of these would be red-green, yellow-purple, and orange-blue.

Square Colors

In a Square color harmony they use a combination of four colors equally spaced around the color wheel. The examples of this would be orange, yellow-green, blue and violet which are all equally spaced apart.

Triadic Colors

A triadic color scheme is comprised of three colors evenly spaced on the color wheel. One example of these colors would be red, blue, and yellow.

Tetradic Colors

The tetradic color scheme uses four colors that are two sets of complements. An example would be, yellow-violet and green-red.

Analogous Colors

Analogous colors are colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. An example of these would be, red, orange, yellow, and green
